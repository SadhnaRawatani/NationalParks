Kirthar National Park

Location: Sindh, Pakistan

Established: 1974

Area: 2,750 square kilometers (1,063 square miles)

Elevation: 1,200 meters (3,900 feet) above sea level

Park Features: Mountains, wildlife, and arid landscapes.

Activities: Wildlife watching, hiking, and photography.

Park Overview

Kirthar National Park, located in the Sindh province of Pakistan, is the second largest national park in the country, spanning across 2,750 square kilometers. Established in 1974, it is a rich natural reserve that features a rugged mountain terrain and vast arid landscapes. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including the Sindh ibex, leopards, wolves, and a diverse bird species. The Kirthar Range, which forms the core of the park, offers a picturesque setting for hiking and exploring nature's untouched beauty.

Conservation Efforts

Despite the challenges of harsh weather conditions, Kirthar National Park remains an important conservation area. Efforts are focused on preserving the park's unique biodiversity and its fragile ecosystems. The park is managed by the Sindh Wildlife Department, which works to prevent poaching, monitor wildlife populations, and protect the endangered species that call Kirthar home.

Wildlife in Kirthar National Park

The park is home to several species of animals, including:

  • Sindh Ibex: A mountain goat species native to the region, often seen on the rocky cliffs.
  • Leopards: These elusive big cats roam the park's dense forests and hills.
  • Wolves: A common predator in the park, the wolf is an important part of the local ecosystem.
  • Wild Boar: These animals can be found in the park's grasslands and foothills.
  • Desert Fox: Small and elusive, the desert fox is adapted to the arid conditions of the park.
  • Bird Species: Kirthar is home to over 200 species of birds, including the endangered vultures and migratory species.

The park's wildlife is vital for maintaining ecological balance, and Kirthar has become a popular destination for wildlife enthusiasts and conservationists alike.

Related Parks:

Hingol National Park, Cholistan Desert Wildlife Park, Astore Valley

Fun Facts:
  • Kirthar National Park is one of the largest protected areas in Pakistan.
  • The park is home to the endangered Sindh ibex, which is a key species for conservation efforts.
  • Kirthar offers spectacular views of the Kirthar mountain range and is a haven for hikers and photographers.
  • The park has over 200 bird species, making it a birdwatcher's paradise.
  • The wildlife of Kirthar includes some of Pakistan's most iconic and rare species, including leopards and desert foxes.
