Margalla Hills National Park

Location: Islamabad, Pakistan

Established: 1980

Area: 17,386 hectares

Elevation: Up to 1,600 meters above sea level

Park Features: Mountainous terrain, rich biodiversity, and scenic hiking trails.

Activities: Hiking, wildlife watching, birdwatching, photography, and nature walks.

Park Overview

Margalla Hills National Park is a protected area located just outside the capital city of Islamabad, Pakistan. Known for its rugged terrain, the park spans over 17,000 hectares and is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna. It provides an ideal escape from the hustle and bustle of the city, offering numerous hiking trails with stunning views of the surrounding hills, valleys, and Islamabad city. The park also serves as a vital ecological area, protecting species of wildlife and plant life native to the region.

Conservation Efforts

Efforts have been made to protect the natural environment of Margalla Hills National Park, particularly focusing on the preservation of wildlife and their habitats. The park is home to a number of endangered species, and local conservation programs help to monitor biodiversity and reduce poaching. The park is also an essential part of environmental education and awareness initiatives aimed at the community.

Wildlife in Margalla Hills National Park

The park is rich in wildlife, with numerous species of mammals, birds, reptiles, and plants. Some of the notable species found in the park include:

  • Leopard: The elusive leopard is one of the top predators in the park, although sightings are rare due to its nocturnal habits.
  • Rhesus Macaque: These monkeys are common in the park and are often seen along the trails and in the forests.
  • Wild Boar: Wild boars are found in the park, often foraging in the forested areas.
  • Vultures: The park is a habitat for several species of vultures, including the Indian Vulture.
  • Various Birds: The park is a birdwatcher's paradise with species such as the Eurasian jay, common cuckoo, and Indian eagle-owl.
  • Serow: A wild goat species that inhabits the rugged hills and cliffs of the park.

In addition to the wildlife, Margalla Hills National Park also supports a rich variety of plant species, including oak trees, pines, and wildflowers that bloom seasonally.

Related Parks:

Himalaya National Park, Ayub National Park, Tilla Jogian

Fun Facts:
  • Margalla Hills National Park is one of the most accessible national parks in Pakistan, being located just a few kilometers from Islamabad.
  • The park offers over 10 hiking trails, each offering unique views of the surrounding hills and valleys.
  • The park is known for its wide diversity of bird species, making it a favorite spot for birdwatchers.
  • It is one of the best spots to enjoy panoramic views of Islamabad, especially from the top of the Pir Sohawa Trail.
  • The park is also home to a variety of medicinal plants used in traditional healing practices.
