Kishtwar National Park

Location: Jammu & Kashmir, India

Established: 1981

Area: 400 square kilometers

Elevation: 1,700 to 5,400 meters above sea level

Park Features: The park is known for its rich biodiversity, high-altitude meadows, dense forests, and majestic mountains. It also houses the Kishtwar Kailash peak.

Activities: Trekking, wildlife observation, photography, camping.

Park Overview

Kishtwar National Park, located in the Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir, India, is known for its natural beauty and biodiversity. The park is spread across a vast area of 400 square kilometers and is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna. The landscape of the park includes high-altitude meadows, dense coniferous forests, and steep hills, providing an ideal habitat for many species of wildlife. One of the key highlights is the Kishtwar Kailash peak, which is revered by the local population and offers a stunning view from the park.

Conservation Efforts

Kishtwar National Park plays a vital role in the conservation of the region’s biodiversity. The park is home to several endangered species and has become a hub for wildlife conservation. Efforts are made to protect the park’s flora and fauna by combating poaching, ensuring habitat protection, and promoting eco-friendly tourism.

Wildlife in Kishtwar National Park

The park is home to a wide variety of wildlife, including several endangered species. Some of the animals found here include:

  • Snow Leopard: The snow leopard is one of the park’s most iconic residents. These elusive big cats are found in the higher altitudes of the park and are critically endangered.
  • Himalayan Brown Bear: Found in the higher, more remote areas, the Himalayan brown bear is an endangered species that thrives in the park’s environment.
  • Hangul (Kashmir Stag): The Hangul is the only species of red deer native to India and is critically endangered. It can be found in the dense forest areas of the park.
  • Wild Boar: The wild boar is commonly found in the lower altitudes of the park, where it digs for food.
  • Himalayan Ibex: A species of wild goat, the Himalayan ibex is found in the rocky slopes and cliffs of the park.
  • Bird Species: The park is home to a variety of bird species, including the Himalayan Griffon Vulture, Golden Eagle, and several species of pheasants.

In addition to terrestrial wildlife, the park’s alpine meadows provide a habitat for a variety of plant species, including medicinal herbs and wildflowers.

Fun Facts:
  • Kishtwar National Park is known for the stunning Kishtwar Kailash peak, which holds religious significance for the local people.
  • The park is home to one of the highest concentrations of snow leopards in the region.
  • It is one of the most biodiverse areas in Jammu and Kashmir, with over 300 species of plants and more than 100 species of animals.
  • The park is also a hotspot for bird watching, with several rare species of birds, including the Himalayan griffon vulture.
  • It is a popular trekking destination, with routes that lead visitors through pristine alpine meadows and dense forests.
